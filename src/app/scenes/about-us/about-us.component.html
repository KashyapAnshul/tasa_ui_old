The Story of Us

We’ve spent our careers in the non-profit, and Financial Services industries fighting for visibility and inclusion in spaces that benefited from our labor and identity without acknowledging or appreciating the value of our contributions.

We understand what it feels like to work twice as hard, and produce twice as much, while receiving fewer advancement opportunities, or getting paid less for doing the same work as colleagues in similar roles.

We’ve maintained various roles because of economic pressure and obligation, or because we were unsure about finding more desirable opportunities.

This is the story of our founding team. Sadly, this is also the story of many people of color in the workplace. We’re talented, ambitious and driven but often feel undervalued, unwelcomed, invisible and shuttered from the opportunities we desire.

We created TaSA as an oasis of belonging and visibility for people of African, Latinx, Indigenous, and Southeast Asian descent.

Our Objectives

CULTIVATE

The largest single network of Black/African, Latinx, Southeast Asian & Indigenous talent in the world.

ENHANCE

Our members’ employability through access to affordably-priced, in-demand online certificate courses from top Universities.

CONNECT

Our global membership to employment and self-employment opportunities through our best-in-class job board and Freelance Platform.

Our Vision

We want TaSA to be an appealing destination where people with shared identities, shared interests, and shared experiences can connect with each other, find desirable opportunities, and form communities of solidarity and trust.
